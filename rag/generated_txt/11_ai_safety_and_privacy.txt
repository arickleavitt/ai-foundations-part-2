AI Safety And Privacy

AI safety means designing AI systems so they behave reliably and reduce harm. Privacy means protecting sensitive information used by or shown to the system.

In RAG applications, privacy is especially important because users may upload internal documents, customer data, contracts, notes, or personal information.

Basic privacy practices:
- Do not upload secrets unless the system is designed to handle them.
- Store only the data you actually need.
- Limit access to the vector database.
- Remove old data when it is no longer needed.
- Avoid logging sensitive prompts or documents.
- Explain to users how their data is handled.

Safety practices include giving the model clear boundaries, validating outputs, adding human review for important decisions, and refusing tasks that are harmful or unsupported.

AI models can produce wrong answers, biased outputs, or persuasive text that is not true. For high-stakes areas such as medical, legal, financial, or safety-critical decisions, AI should support qualified humans rather than replace them.

Responsible AI development is a process. It includes design, testing, documentation, monitoring, and improvement over time.
