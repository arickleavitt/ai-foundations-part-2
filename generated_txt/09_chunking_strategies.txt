Chunking Strategies For RAG

Chunking is the process of splitting documents into smaller pieces before embedding them. Good chunking improves retrieval quality.

If chunks are too small, they may lose important context. If chunks are too large, retrieval may return broad text that contains unrelated information.

A common beginner strategy is fixed-size chunking. For example, split text into chunks of about 800 to 1,200 characters with some overlap between chunks. Overlap helps preserve context across chunk boundaries.

More advanced strategies split by structure. Markdown documents can be split by headings. PDFs can be split by page or section. Code can be split by function or class.

Chunking choices depend on the documents and user questions. A legal document, a product manual, a research paper, and a chat transcript may each need a different approach.

Useful metadata for chunks:
- Source filename.
- Chunk index.
- Page number.
- Section heading.
- Document title.
- Timestamp or version.

The best chunking strategy is usually found through testing. Ask real questions, inspect retrieved chunks, and adjust chunk size and overlap.
