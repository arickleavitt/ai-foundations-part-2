Machine Learning Basics

Machine learning is a major part of artificial intelligence. It is the process of training a computer model to find patterns in data and use those patterns to make predictions or decisions.

A simple example is email spam detection. A machine learning model can be trained on many examples of spam and non-spam emails. Over time, it learns signals that help it decide whether a new email looks suspicious.

There are several common types of machine learning:

Supervised learning uses labeled examples. For example, a dataset might include customer messages labeled as positive, negative, or neutral.

Unsupervised learning looks for patterns without labels. For example, it might group similar customers together based on behavior.

Reinforcement learning trains systems through rewards and penalties. It is often used in games, robotics, and decision-making tasks.

Machine learning workflows usually include collecting data, cleaning data, training a model, testing the model, and monitoring performance after deployment.

Good machine learning depends on good data. Poor data quality can lead to unreliable predictions, biased results, or models that fail in real-world use.
