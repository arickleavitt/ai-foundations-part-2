Evaluating AI Outputs

AI applications should be evaluated before they are trusted. Evaluation means checking whether outputs are accurate, useful, safe, and consistent.

For a RAG app, evaluation often starts with a test set of questions and expected answers. The developer asks the app each question, reviews the answer, and checks whether the retrieved sources support the response.

Important evaluation questions:
- Did the app retrieve the right chunks?
- Did the model answer using the retrieved context?
- Did the answer include unsupported claims?
- Did the app say "I do not know" when the documents lacked the answer?
- Was the answer clear for the intended user?

Evaluation can be manual, automated, or both. Manual review is useful early in development. Automated checks are helpful when the app changes frequently.

Metrics for retrieval include precision, recall, and relevance. Metrics for answer quality include correctness, groundedness, helpfulness, and formatting consistency.

AI systems can drift as documents, prompts, models, and user behavior change. Ongoing monitoring helps catch problems after launch.
