Prompt Engineering Basics

Prompt engineering is the practice of writing clear instructions for an AI model. A prompt can include a task, background information, examples, constraints, and the desired output format.

A weak prompt might say: "Tell me about this."

A stronger prompt might say: "Summarize the following article in five bullet points for a beginner audience. Include the main idea, two supporting details, and one open question."

Good prompts are specific. They tell the model what role to play, what input to use, what output to produce, and what to avoid.

Useful prompt patterns:
- Define the task clearly.
- Provide relevant context.
- Specify the audience.
- Ask for a format, such as bullets, JSON, a table, or a short paragraph.
- Include examples when consistency matters.
- Tell the model what to do when information is missing.

Prompt engineering is not magic. It works best when paired with good data, retrieval, evaluation, and application logic.
